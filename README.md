# HR-Analytics-Dashboard
Here is a short summary of the key insights from each sheet:
1.  **HR Raw Data:** The foundational dataset for attrition analysis, including demographic, job, and compensation details, plus derived fields like **Tenure Bond** and **AI Impact Score**.
2.  **Pivot 1 (Job Role vs. Attrition):** **Research Scientists** have the highest attrition rate (**$33.22\%$**), while **Laboratory Technicians** have the lowest (**$23.94\%$**).
3.  **Pivot 2 (Tenure vs. Job Satisfaction):** Across all tenure groups (0-2, 3-6, 7+ years), the majority of employees who left (attrited) reported a **high Job Satisfaction (levels 3 and 4)**, suggesting other factors (like pay or career path) are driving departures.
4.  **Pivot 3 (Income vs. Performance Rating):** High-performing employees (**Performance Rating 4**) in the **Low Income** bracket are the most at risk, representing **$17.47\%$** of leavers in that group, higher than in Medium or High income groups.
5.  **Pivot 4 (AI Impact Score vs. Attrition):** Roles with a moderate-to-high **AI Impact Score of 3** have the lowest attrition rate (**$10.61\%$**), while those with the **lowest score (1)** have the highest (**$18.56\%$**).
